Top 10 Food Spots in Kolkata

Local favorites and bustling markets, plus seasonal highlights and crowd tips for the ultimate culinary tour.

Seasonal Highlights & Crowd Tips

Kolkata’s food scene is vibrant year-round, but timing can enhance your experience:

  • Winter (Nov–Feb): Best time for street food; cool weather keeps crowds comfortable.
  • Spring (Mar–Apr): Perfect for fresh Bengali sweets during local festivals.
  • Monsoon (Jul–Sep): Romantic rains pair well with chai and pakoras; expect some market delays.
  • Durga Puja (Sept–Oct): Streets are lively and packed; incredible festive delicacies abound.
Tip: Visit early evenings to avoid long queues and to see food being freshly prepared.

Top 10 Must-Try Food Spots

  1. Indian Coffee House (College Street): Iconic for adda sessions and classic coffee.
  2. Balwant Singh’s Eating House: Famous for doodh cola and traditional snacks.
  3. Peter Cat (Park Street): Renowned for Chelo Kebab platters.
  4. Nizam’s (New Market): Birthplace of the legendary Kolkata Kathi Roll.
  5. Mitra Café (Shobhabazar): Historic spot for fish fry and kabiraji cutlet.
  6. Bhojohori Manna: Authentic Bengali thali with homely flavors.
  7. 6 Ballygunge Place: Heritage dining for refined Bengali cuisine.
  8. Flurys (Park Street): Classic tearoom for pastries and English breakfast.
  9. Teritti Bazaar: Morning Chinese breakfast street market.
  10. Dakshinapan Market: Regional snacks and handicrafts in a laid-back courtyard.

Extra Tips for Food Lovers

  • Carry cash; smaller stalls may not accept cards.
  • Be adventurous—try puchka, jhalmuri, and other street-side delights.
  • Stay hydrated and pace yourself; the variety can be overwhelming!
